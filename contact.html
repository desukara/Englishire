Before You Begin

The useful details are usually the practical ones.

There is no need to compose an elaborate account. A clear outline of the school, the timetable, the learners and the work required is considerably more useful.

Urgent circumstances do not always allow every answer to be known at once. In such cases, kindly send what is available and make clear which details remain to be confirmed.

The more complete the information, the more efficiently we can consider whether a suitable teacher may be provided.

An enquiry is not a confirmation. Every assignment must first be reviewed properly.

Before Sending the Form

A few final practical questions.

These answers concern the enquiry itself. The full questions page contains further information about temporary teacher cover.

What if the timetable is not yet final?

Provide the current version and make clear that it remains provisional. Any material change must be reviewed before cover can be confirmed.

May we send supporting documents separately?

Yes. Timetables, lesson plans, school maps and other useful documents may be supplied after the initial enquiry, subject to the communication arrangements provided in our response.

Does submitting the form reserve a teacher?

No. Submission begins the review process. No teacher is reserved or confirmed until Englishire has expressly agreed the assignment.

Can we enquire before the need becomes urgent?

Certainly. Advance introductions are welcome and may make a future request rather more straightforward.
