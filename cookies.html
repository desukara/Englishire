Cookies

Small technologies deserve a clear explanation.

This page explains the cookies and similar technologies which may help the Englishire website function properly.

Cookies need not be mysterious. They are simply small pieces of information used by websites and browsers for purposes such as security, preferences, forms and basic measurement.

Last reviewed: 16 July 2026

The Straightforward Explanation

The website should use only what it has a reason to use.

Some cookies are essential because a website cannot operate sensibly without them. Others may be used to remember a choice, understand basic usage or support a service provided by another organisation.

Englishire does not regard the existence of a technology as a sufficient reason to deploy it. Non-essential tools should be used only where they serve a clear purpose.

Necessity should come before convenience.

01

What cookies are

A cookie is a small text file which a website may ask a browser to store on a computer, telephone or other device.

Cookies can help a website remember information between pages or visits. Similar technologies may perform related functions without using a traditional cookie file.

A cookie is not, by itself, a programme and cannot read every file on a device. Its significance depends upon the information placed within it and the purpose for which it is used.

02

Essential cookies

Essential cookies are used where the website requires them for basic operation, security or a function requested by the visitor.

They may, for example, assist with:

  • secure and reliable page delivery;
  • fraud, abuse or security protection;
  • remembering a temporary form or session state;
  • maintaining basic website preferences;
  • supporting a cookie-choice mechanism itself.

Because these technologies support the service requested by the visitor, the website may not function properly if they are blocked.

03

Preference cookies

Preference cookies may remember a choice such as language, display setting, accessibility preference or another option selected by the visitor.

Englishire should use such cookies only where a remembered choice provides a genuine benefit rather than merely adding complexity.

04

Performance and analytics

Analytics technologies may help a website understand broad usage patterns, such as which pages are visited, whether visitors encounter technical difficulties and how the site might be made clearer or more useful.

If Englishire introduces non-essential analytics, the relevant service should be identified, the purpose should be explained and an appropriate choice or consent mechanism should be provided where required.

Analytics should not be described as anonymous where the technology or provider can reasonably associate the information with an individual, device or account.

05

Third-party services

Some website functions may be supplied by other organisations, such as hosting providers, form services, embedded media, security tools or analytics providers.

Those services may set their own cookies or receive technical information when their content or function is used.

Englishire should identify material third-party tools within this policy or an accompanying cookie notice when they are introduced. The provider's own terms and privacy information may also apply.

06

Managing cookies

Most browsers allow cookies to be viewed, deleted or blocked. The precise controls differ between browsers and devices.

Visitors may also be presented with an Englishire cookie choice panel where non-essential technologies require a decision.

Refusing non-essential cookies should not prevent access to the ordinary informational pages of the website. Blocking essential cookies, however, may affect forms, security or other requested functions.

07

Current use on this website

At the review date shown above, the website code supplied by Englishire does not set cookies or use browser storage. It does not include analytics, advertising trackers or embedded third-party media.

The enquiry form prepares a message in the visitor's own email application. The website itself does not transmit or store the form entries.

A hosting provider may keep ordinary server access logs. If Englishire later introduces cookies, analytics, embedded media or another service, this policy and any required consent controls should be updated before that technology is enabled.

08

Privacy

Cookies form part of the wider manner in which information may be handled through the website.

The Privacy Policy explains the types of information Englishire may receive, the purposes for which it may be used and the choices available to individuals.

09

Changes to this policy

This policy may be revised when the website, its service providers, its technology or applicable requirements change.

The review date at the top of the page should be updated when a meaningful change is made.

10

Contact
