Why the Journal Exists

School continuity is built through decisions that are rarely visible when they are made well.

The Englishire Journal examines the systems, judgements and human consequences behind staffing, recruitment, teacher turnover and classroom continuity.

These subjects are often discussed as though they belonged entirely to administration. In practice, they shape the confidence of families, the workload of colleagues, the stability of classrooms and the long-term health of the school itself.

Volume I brings together eight publications written to stand on their own, whilst forming a connected account of how English education in Japan is changing and how thoughtful schools may respond.

Eight publications. One continuing argument: continuity is not an accident.

Featured Essay · The Industry

The End of the Easy Teacher

Japan's English schools spent twenty years hiring people who wanted to come to Japan—and are only now discovering that this was never the same thing as hiring teachers.

The candidate pool may appear larger than ever, yet the number of people a school can employ with confidence is a rather smaller category. This essay examines the formalisation of hiring, the limits of old assumptions and the difference between popularity and genuine leverage.

Editorial Position

The Journal is intended to be read beyond the moment of publication.

We do not publish to fill a calendar, satisfy an algorithm or manufacture an opinion where none is required.

Each publication should answer a meaningful question, remain useful beyond the week in which it appears and leave the reader with something worth considering.

We hope Volume I proves useful to schools, fair to teachers and worthy of the reader's time.
