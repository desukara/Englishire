How to Read This Page

General guidance should clarify, not overpromise.

A school asking about one morning of cover is not presenting the same requirement as a school seeking several weeks of recurring lessons. The age of the learners, the timetable, the journey, the materials and the responsibilities involved may alter the answer considerably.

The guidance on this page therefore describes the usual Englishire approach. It explains what may be possible, what information is commonly required and where a matter must be considered individually.

Where another page gives the fuller explanation, a reference is provided. The service page explains what Englishire offers, How It Works describes the process and The Englishire Standard sets out the principles beneath those decisions.

An answer on this page is guidance. An assignment becomes definite only when it has been expressly reviewed and confirmed.

Chapter I

About Englishire

The organisation, its purpose and the nature of the service.

What does Englishire do?

Englishire provides temporary English teacher cover for schools and education providers throughout Tokyo. The service is intended for absences, recruitment gaps, additional capacity, leave, events and other circumstances in which lessons require a capable teacher for a limited period.

See Teacher Cover for the fuller service description.

Why does temporary teacher cover matter?

Proper temporary cover protects continuity whilst a school deals with the wider staffing matter. It allows lessons to continue without forcing a hurried permanent appointment or leaving existing colleagues to absorb every affected class.

Is Englishire a permanent recruitment agency?

The principal service described on this website is temporary teacher cover. Temporary cover and permanent recruitment serve different purposes and should not be presented as though they were interchangeable.

Where does Englishire work?

Enquiries may be considered across Tokyo and, where practical, nearby areas. The address, nearest station, timetable and realistic journey must be reviewed before an assignment can be accepted.

What principles guide the service?

The central principles include suitability before convenience, preparation before placement, communication before assumption and respect for the established school.

They are set out in full in The Englishire Standard.

Chapter II

Before Contacting Englishire

Notice, preparation and what a school should gather in advance.

How much notice should a school provide?

As much notice as circumstances reasonably allow. More notice generally provides more options, particularly where the timetable is complex, the journey is substantial or the assignment requires particular experience.

Are urgent enquiries welcome?

Yes. Sudden illness and family emergencies rarely provide courteous notice. Urgency, however, does not remove the need to understand the location, timetable, learners and essential responsibilities.

May a school introduce itself before cover is needed?

Certainly. A prior introduction can be useful, particularly where a school expects recurring requirements or has a timetable that benefits from advance understanding.

What information is most useful at first contact?

The school name, address, nearest station, requested dates, reporting time, lesson timetable, learner ages, class sizes, lesson format, materials and any particular requirements.

What if some information is not yet known?

Send what is available and identify what remains uncertain. Englishire can then explain which details are essential before the arrangement can be reviewed or confirmed.

The full sequence is described in How It Works.

Chapter III

Teacher Cover

The likely duration, shape and continuity of an assignment.

Can a teacher cover just one day?

Yes. Cover may concern a single day, several consecutive days, recurring weekly lessons or a longer temporary period, subject to suitability and availability.

How long may an assignment last?

There is no single standard duration. Longer assignments usually require fuller arrangements concerning materials, learner progress, reporting, communication and handover.

Can Englishire help during a recruitment gap?

Potentially, yes. Temporary cover can allow the school to continue lessons whilst conducting permanent recruitment with the care that appointment deserves.

Can the same teacher return for recurring cover?

This may be possible and is often desirable where continuity matters. It depends upon the teacher's availability, the timetable and whether the assignment remains substantially as first agreed.

Does an enquiry guarantee that cover is available?

No. An enquiry begins the review process. Cover is confirmed only where the assignment has been understood and a suitable teacher is available.

Chapter IV

Schools and Learners

The environments, age groups and programmes which may be considered.

Which kinds of organisation may enquire?

Enquiries may be considered from language schools, private and international programmes, after-school education providers, academies, corporate programmes and related educational organisations.

Which age groups may be supported?

Children, teenagers, university-age learners and adults may all be considered. The age group matters because the same teacher is not necessarily suitable for every classroom.

Does Englishire support children's programmes?

Potentially, yes. Young learner classes require warmth, pace, clear boundaries and sensible classroom command. Suitability should be considered with particular care.

Does Englishire support adult or specialist programmes?

Potentially, yes. The learner profile, aims, materials and expected teaching approach must first be understood.

Why is the school environment relevant?

Every school has its own routines, expectations and manner of communicating with learners, parents and staff. A temporary teacher should support that environment rather than arrive determined to replace it.

Chapter V

Teachers

Suitability, briefing and the conduct expected within the school.

How is teacher suitability considered?

Suitability is considered in relation to learner ages, lesson format, school setting, timetable, journey, duration and the responsibilities of the assignment. Availability is necessary, but it is not the whole answer.

May a school request particular experience?

Yes. Genuine requirements concerning age groups, lesson types, qualifications or specialist experience should be explained at the outset. Such requirements may affect availability.

What should the teacher know before arrival?

The teacher should understand the address, nearest station, reporting point, timetable, learner groups, objectives, materials, dress expectations and relevant school procedures.

Should a temporary teacher follow the school's methods?

Yes, where those methods have been explained and are appropriate to the assignment. The teacher enters an established environment and should support its routines rather than impose a personal system upon it.

What professional standards are expected?

Punctuality, preparation, appropriate dress, sensible communication, respectful conduct, confidentiality and proper attention to learners are fundamental expectations.

See The Englishire Standard for the fuller account.

Chapter VI

Lessons and Materials

Plans, objectives, resources and continuity between teachers.

Should the school provide lesson plans?

Where lesson plans already exist, they are extremely useful. They help the temporary teacher preserve the direction of the course and avoid unnecessary repetition.

What if no full lesson plan exists?

The school should provide the clearest available account of the objective, materials, work already completed and what the lesson should achieve. Any preparation expected from the teacher should be discussed before confirmation.

Who supplies teaching materials?

This depends upon the assignment. Schools commonly provide textbooks, worksheets, plans or access to existing resources. Any expectation that the teacher should create or bring materials must be stated clearly.

Can the teacher continue work already begun?

Yes, provided sufficient information is supplied concerning what has been completed, what should follow and any work the returning teacher will expect to receive.

Can homework, assessment or lesson reports be included?

Potentially, yes. The expectations and time required should be explained before the assignment is confirmed, particularly where cover extends beyond a single day.

Chapter VII

Review and Confirmation

What happens after the school sends its requirement.

How does a school begin an enquiry?

The school may use the enquiry page and provide the practical information available at the time.

What happens after the enquiry is sent?

Englishire reviews the school, timetable, journey, learners, lesson expectations, duration and teacher suitability. Further questions may be asked before any answer is given.

Does submitting the form reserve a teacher?

No. Submission begins the review process. No teacher is reserved or confirmed merely because a form has been received.

How is confirmation provided?

Confirmation should identify the agreed dates, timetable, location, principal responsibilities and any relevant practical terms. Until confirmation is expressly given, the enquiry remains under consideration.

Why might further questions be necessary?

A missing detail may affect punctuality, lesson preparation, learner suitability or the responsibilities expected of the teacher. Clarification before confirmation is preferable to negotiation at the school gate.

See How It Works.

Chapter VIII

Practical Matters

Travel, arrival and the arrangements surrounding the first lesson.

Why is the nearest station important?

Tokyo journeys depend upon more than the address. Railway lines, transfers, walking time and reporting arrangements all affect whether the teacher can arrive punctually.

How early should the teacher arrive?

The reporting time should be agreed in advance and allow for sign-in, introductions, locating rooms, collecting materials and receiving any final briefing.

Should the school explain the dress code?

Yes. Even where the expectation appears obvious to the school, a brief statement prevents unnecessary uncertainty.

What access information should be supplied?

The entrance, floor, reception or security procedure, contact person and any difficulty locating the building should be mentioned.

Should safeguarding or emergency procedures be explained?

Yes. The teacher should receive the procedures relevant to the assignment and know whom to contact if a concern arises.

Chapter IX

Administration

Fees, changes, records and matters which should be agreed rather than assumed.

How are fees determined?

Fees depend upon the assignment, duration, timetable, location, preparation required and other practical considerations. The applicable arrangement should be agreed before confirmation.

How is transport treated?

The treatment of transport should be made clear as part of the agreed arrangement. Precise location information is necessary so that travel can be considered realistically.

What if the timetable changes after confirmation?

Englishire should be informed promptly. A material change to dates, times, location, learner groups or responsibilities may affect suitability, availability or the agreed terms.

May a school cancel or shorten an assignment?

Any cancellation or reduction should be discussed promptly and handled according to the terms agreed for that assignment.

How is personal information handled?

Personal information is handled according to the Privacy Policy. Schools should avoid sending sensitive learner information unless it is genuinely required and appropriate to share.

Chapter X

During the Assignment

Communication, changes and what happens when circumstances move.

What if the school's needs change during the assignment?

Englishire should be informed. A modest practical adjustment may be straightforward; a substantial change in timetable, location, learner group or responsibility may require the arrangement to be reconsidered.

How should concerns be communicated?

Promptly, clearly and in proportion to the matter. A concern is easier to address whilst the relevant details remain current.

May the school provide updated materials?

Certainly. Updated materials, objectives or notes should be supplied in sufficient time for the teacher to use them properly.

What if the absence lasts longer than expected?

Further dates may be considered, though an extension is not automatic. Availability, timetable changes and the continuing responsibilities should be reviewed and confirmed.

Chapter XI

After the Assignment

Handover, feedback and the value of preserving what was learned.

Should the teacher provide a handover?

Where relevant, yes. The appropriate handover may range from a brief note concerning work completed to a fuller record of learner progress, homework, materials and outstanding matters.

May a school provide feedback?

Certainly. Clear and fair feedback is useful and may assist with future arrangements.

Can an assignment be extended?

Potentially, yes. An extension depends upon availability and should be discussed and confirmed rather than assumed.

Can the same teacher be requested again?

A school may certainly ask. Whether the same teacher can return depends upon availability and the nature of the new requirement.

Why maintain contact after a successful assignment?

A prior understanding of the school, timetable and expectations can make a future enquiry more straightforward, particularly where cover requirements recur.

Chapter XII · When We May Decline

Occasionally, no is the more professional answer.

Declining an assignment does not suggest that the requirement is unimportant. It means that Englishire is not satisfied the work can be undertaken to an appropriate standard under the circumstances presented.

A prompt and honest answer is preferable to an uncertain promise which later places the school, teacher or learners in difficulty.

  • Essential information is unavailable The assignment cannot be understood sufficiently to make a responsible decision.
  • No suitably matched teacher is free Availability alone does not establish suitability.
  • The journey is not dependable Punctual attendance must remain realistic in ordinary conditions.
  • The work requires different expertise Specialist expectations deserve an appropriately experienced teacher.
  • The arrangement cannot meet the required standard Urgency should not compel a promise that cannot be honoured properly.
Clear answers are valuable. Clear boundaries are equally so.
